Them Photos

An image gallery app on Android for photography enthusiasts.

Overview

The project was initiated by exploring the possibilities in the cloud storage market. Deciding which industry to explore was difficult, it is vast. I created a framework to narrow it down. I looked into popular cloud service providers with a common service. It turned out to be photos. The Them Photos is a cloud storage geared at photography enthusiasts. This app will be a tool that takes it a step further by allowing users to control their organization of photos by having access to the features with a different approach to the interface.

Problem

Users had minimal time invested into photos being stored. There was no sense of value for the product that catered to a general audience. As robust as the apps were, they lacked access to tools for organzing photos.

The Solution

Creating an interface that is transparent to access features to complete tasks in an app will add value to a target market therefore retaining them onto one platform.

Innovation Intent

To create a cloud photo storage space for photographers, creatives with capabilities to collaborate with others, develop a network/community and help with a marketing platform to connect and share images.

Branding

Ahh Moment

Creating the name of the app While doing word associations with photography, ‘them’ would come up. What do people do with them? What do I do with them? Out of a little frustration, I said ‘..photos, what do you do with them, Them Photos.’

Concept

I utilized word associations and mind mapping to develope a concept. Words that resonated were ‘structure and organization'. Boxes of analog photography items and colors inspired by transparent contrast filters sparked the direction of the brand.

Usability Testing

After the usability testing on the wireframe clickable I wanted to focus on three tasks to tighten the interface with the branding. The results were not as successful in the menu set up. Typography lacked hierarchy structure. The users were unsure where to go first at boarding that was associated with uncertainty if they were working off their phone or on the cloud server.

  • Visual cues between elements were distinguishable
  • Focus on sign-up to sharing helped execute faster iterations
  • Find a pattern for photo views selection and task bar placement
  • Add interface element for user control and affordances
  • Add Android bottom nav bar
  • Clean up visual design -typeface and color require more attention
  • Look into Material Design for patterns to help with chunking and familiarity

A/B Testing

A quick preference test to get insight to color preferences. This assisted in focusing my efforts to create minimal color on the next High Fidity.

Improvements

I took another look at patterns in Material Design for solutions to interface patterns. Also to add familiarity to Them Photos App.

    1. Menu top bar and bottom at empty state
    2. Typography changed to Roboto for familiarity
    3. Interface points to being on the cloud service
    4. Android bottom Nav Bar
    1. The arrow to go back to the Dashboard
    2. Tab menu for different views
    3. Android bottom task menu bar
    4. Minimized color and typeface variations
    1. Floating action button made it clearer to select an action
    2. Task fab button finalizes task or continues onto another in feature

Conclusion

Expereimenting with the interface by chunking tasks is the beginning to a simplified interface. There are people that want to have control over their photos. Have access by having the features on their task bar.

Takeaways

  • Material Design’s UI System is a good place to begin looking at familiar interface patterns
  • The Floating Actionable Button really helped in refining chunking of tasks
  • Creating a clickable wireframe allowed me to work out solutions for UI developement
